How to Pack Efficiently for Your Storage Unit

By Dwayne Grubbs Owner at G3 Storage
Published: May 15, 2023

Whether you're using a mobile storage container or one of our on-site units, how you pack your belongings can make a significant difference in maximizing space, protecting your items, and making future access easier. Here are our expert tips for packing your storage unit efficiently.

Start With the Right Supplies

Using proper packing materials is the foundation of efficient storage:

  • Uniform boxes: When possible, use similarly sized, sturdy boxes. They'll stack better and reduce wasted space.
  • Quality packing tape: Don't skimp here—cheap tape can lose its adhesiveness over time.
  • Furniture covers: Protect furniture with proper covers rather than plastic sheeting, which can trap moisture.
  • Mattress bags: Always use proper mattress bags to prevent stains and damage.
  • Labels and markers: Label every box clearly on multiple sides.

Create an Inventory System

Before you start packing, create a simple inventory system:

  1. Number each box
  2. Create a spreadsheet or list documenting what's in each box
  3. Consider color-coding boxes by room or category
  4. Take photos of valuable items before packing

This extra step will save you countless hours of searching later when you need to find specific items.

Pro Tip

Keep a digital copy of your inventory in cloud storage, and tape a paper copy to the inside of your storage unit door for quick reference.

Pack Strategically

How you pack each box matters:

  • Place heavier items at the bottom of boxes
  • Fill empty spaces with packing paper or bubble wrap to prevent shifting
  • Avoid overloading boxes—if you can't lift it easily, it's too heavy
  • Disassemble furniture when possible to save space
  • Store items you'll need access to near the front of the unit

Create a Loading Plan

Before you start loading your storage unit, create a plan:

  1. Large, heavy items first: Place large furniture and appliances against the back wall.
  2. Create a center aisle: Leave a walkway down the middle for access to items in the back.
  3. Stack boxes by weight: Heavier boxes on the bottom, lighter ones on top.
  4. Use vertical space: Stack items safely to maximize your cubic footage.
  5. Frequently accessed items in front: Keep seasonal decorations, important documents, or other items you'll need to access toward the entrance.

Items That Need Special Attention

Electronics

Store electronics in their original packaging when possible. If not available, use anti-static bubble wrap and avoid plastic bags which can trap moisture. Remove batteries before storage.

Appliances

Clean and dry thoroughly. Leave doors slightly ajar to prevent mold and odors. Tape cords to the sides of the appliances to prevent damage.

Furniture

Apply furniture polish to wooden surfaces before storage to add moisture protection. Cover upholstered items with cotton sheets rather than plastic, which can trap humidity.

Clothing and Fabrics

Clean all items before storage—stains can set permanently over time. Use wardrobe boxes for hanging clothes and cedar blocks instead of mothballs.

Climate Considerations in New Bern

Our coastal Carolina climate presents unique challenges for storage. High humidity can damage sensitive items, so consider these additional tips:

  • Choose climate-controlled storage for valuable or sensitive items
  • Use moisture absorbers in non-climate-controlled units
  • Elevate items off the floor using pallets
  • Leave space between items and walls for air circulation

Humidity Tip

Place silica gel packets in boxes containing books, photos, and important documents to absorb excess moisture.

Security Considerations

While our facilities at G3 Storage offer excellent security with 24/7 surveillance, you can take additional steps to protect your belongings:

  • Use disc locks rather than padlocks when possible
  • Don't store valuables like jewelry or important documents
  • Consider insurance for valuable items
  • Keep an updated inventory with photos of valuable items

Final Thoughts

Taking time to pack properly will save you headaches later. Whether you're storing items for a few weeks during a move or for several months during a renovation, these principles will help keep your belongings safe and accessible.
